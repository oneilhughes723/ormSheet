Risk Management (RM) Instructions:


Crews will fill out the required information on the top of the RM card. The Ops SUP and duty dog will initial the "SUP" and "Logged" blocks respectively. If an O&B, crews will fill out two ORM sheets, one with their scheduled takeoff time and another with their planned takeoff time out of the outbase. Assign a point value to each row of risk factors as appropriate, the highest value for each row will be recorded. For Health & Stress/Fatigue, use the matricies below. Each crew member will tally their points and determine their overall score rating, the highest member on the crew will drive the value taken on the RM card. (e.g. a crew member scores 5 on fatigue which correlates to a MODERATE, this will drive a MODERATE on the RM card) Tally all scores on the RM card to determine appropriate level.

* Health and Stress Scoring
  • Any Factor HIGH = Overall Score High
  • Total of all four factors 3 or more = Overall Score HIGH
  • Total of all four factors 1-2 = Overall Score MODERATE
  • Total of all four factors 0 = Overall Score LOW
 ** Fatigue Scoring:
  • Any Factor HIGH = Overall Score HIGH
  • Total of all five factors 7 or more = Overall HIGH
  • Total of all five factos 4-6 = Overall Score MODERATE
  • Total of all five factors 1-3 = Overall Score LOW


CREW RESOURCE MANAGEMENT (CRM) GUIDE


# CRM SKILL POSITIVE FACTORS NEGATIVE FACTORS
1 Mission Analysis Adequate pre-mission analysis & planning/briefing. Ongoing mission evaluation using Threat & Error Management tools & techniques. Effective post mission debrief. Includes crewmember responses & outcomes to threats and errors. Brief: Neglects, rushed incomplete, vague, ignores. Ongoing Mission: Fails to conduct timely threat/ error management. Debrief: Rushed, incomplete, vague, blames, ignores.
2 Situational Awareness Anticipates, identifies errors, prevents loss, recognizes own/others' loss & uses techniques for recovering from loss. Disoriented, confused, lost, fixated
3 Crew/Flight Coordination Flight/mission integrity, maintains contracts, team-building, leadership, responsibility, assertiveness, persistence, conflict resolution & solution driven recommendations/decisions. Lacks flight discipline, judges, ridicules, over-reacts, ignores, imposes, accepts error.
4 Communication Listens, provides feedback, precision and efficiency of communication with all members & agencies (i.e., crewmembers, wingmen, weather, ATC, Intelligence, etc.). Interrupts, withholds, discounts, ambiguous, unclear, incomplete, or inaccurate
5 Task Management Establishes priorities, manages automation & available resources, checklist discipline, & standard operating procedures. Rushed, overloaded, complacent, mis-prioritizes.
6 Risk Managment/Decision Making Uses risk management processes, problem solving, evaluation of hazards, deliberate, real time, and correct decisions. Avoids decisions, delays, wavers, argues, fails to evaluate consequences of decisions.
